Modern architecture demands sophisticated information management. We utilize **BIM (Building Information Modeling)** methodology to create shared digital models that enable full cooperation between clients, designers, and contractors, optimizing timelines and reducing unforeseen issues during the construction phase.

In alignment with the current regulatory framework—which, as of **2025**, mandates the use of electronic modeling methods for all new works—we integrate digitalization into every phase of the building's lifecycle.

### 🔄 Integrated Process Phases
* **Site Survey and Analysis:** Accurate mapping of current conditions and preliminary site analysis for new constructions or renovation projects.
* **Information Modeling:** Creation of a digital exchange model, the core of collaboration between various disciplines (structural, MEP, architectural).
* **Collaborative Design:** Simultaneous coordination with structural and systems engineers to ensure interference-free integration during the design stage.
* **Execution Monitoring and As-Built:** Continuous updating of the model during the construction phase to reflect real-world progress, culminating in the delivery of the final **As-Built** model.
* **Facility Management:** Leveraging the information model for strategic maintenance planning and efficient long-term building management.

### 📐 A Multidisciplinary Approach
Through BIM, we transform architectural vision into a navigable digital prototype. Every component (walls, windows, systems) contains precise technical data, facilitating rigorous control over costs and project sustainability.
